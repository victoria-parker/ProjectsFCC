Amelia Earhart

The first woman to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean

Here's a time line of Amelia Earhart's life:

  • 1897 - Born in Atchison, Kansas on July 24
  • 1908 - Amelia saw an airplane for the first time at the Iowa State Fair
  • 1913 - Amelia entered Central High School
  • 1914 - Amelia entered Hyde Park High School
  • 1915 - Amelia graduated from Hyde Park High School in Chicago in June 1915
  • 1916 - Amelia entered college in October 1916, attending the Ogontz School near Philadelphia
  • 1918 - She became a Voluntary Aid Detachment nurse at the Spadina Military Convalescent Hospital in Toronto, caring for wounded World War I soldiers
  • 1919 - Amelia returned to the United States to live with her mother and sister in Northampton, Massachusetts. She enrolled as a pre-med student at Columbia University in New York
  • 1920 - Amelia attended her first air meet, at Daugherty Field in Long Beach
  • 1922 - Amelia was pictured in the Los Angeles Examiner with her Kinner airplane
  • 1923 - he was granted her airline pilot’s license by the Fédération Aéronautique Internationale
  • 1924 - Amelia bought her second Kinner airplane, which she shortly thereafter sold to buy a Kissel roadster car she called the “Yellow Peril.”
  • 1925 - In May 1925, Amelia left Columbia and returned to the Boston area
  • 1926 - Amelia began working part-time as a social worker at Denison House
  • 1927 - Amelia became a full-time resident staff member at Denison House in the autumn of 1927, and was also elected Secretary to the Board of Directors
  • 1928 - Amelia received a phone call while working at Denison House. The caller, Captain Hilton H. Railey, asked Amelia if she would like to be the first woman to fly across the Atlantic. After checking his references, Amelia enthusiastically agreed to the adventure
  • 1929 - Amelia acquired a single engine Lockheed Vega airplane. She competed in the Women's Air Derby race from Santa Monica to Cleveland (also called the “Powder-Puff Derby”), the first cross-country race for women, finishing in third place
  • 1930 - Amelia accepted a public relations job with Pennsylvania Railroad
  • 1931 - Amelia was named the first president of The Ninety-Nines, the first women pilots’ organization (1931-1933)
  • 1932 - In May, despite a failed altimeter, dense fog, and a fire from her exhaust manifold, Amelia became the first woman (and second person) to fly solo across the Atlantic Ocean.
  • 1933 - Amelia visited the White House as a guest of Franklin and Eleanor Roosevelt
  • 1934 - Amelia launched a fashion house to manufacture and market clothing designed by her
  • 1935 - Amelia became the first person to fly solo across the Pacific Ocean from Honolulu, Hawaii to Oakland, California (January 11-12)
  • 1936 - Amelia and Albert Einstein spoke at the opening of the New York Museum of Science and Industry. Amelia testified before a Senate sub-committee on air safety, campaigned for the 1936 Democratic Party, and was honored by women geographers that same year
  • 1937 - To help finance Amelia’s world flight, George Putnam arranged for Gimbels in New York to sell letter covers that Amelia would carry with her, and, along the route, mail back to collectors. Ten thousand of the covers sold. Amelia began her round-the-world flight at the equator in Oakland, California and set a new record for fastest east to west (Oakland to Honolulu) travel in 15 hours and 47 minutes (March 17-18)
  • 1939 - Amelia Earhart was declared legally dead in superior court in Los Angeles, CA (january 5)
